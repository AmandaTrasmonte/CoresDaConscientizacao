Red December — HIV/AIDS Awareness Month

About

Red December is a Brazilian awareness campaign on HIV/AIDS and other STIs, held since 2017. The campaign reinforces the importance of prevention, early diagnosis, treatment, and protection of the rights of people living with HIV.

December was chosen because it includes World AIDS Day on December 1st.

What is Red December?

  • It is a national campaign to prevent HIV/AIDS and other sexually transmitted infections (STIs).
  • Established by Law No. 13.504/2017.
  • The red color symbolizes the ribbon representing the fight against AIDS and solidarity.

Campaign Objectives

  • Provide information on HIV and STI transmission, treatment, and prevention.
  • Encourage protective habits, such as using condoms.
  • Reduce stigma and discrimination.
  • Ensure access to proper diagnosis and treatment.

How to Participate

  • Learn about transmission and prevention of STIs.
  • Use condoms in all sexual relations.
  • Talk to friends and family about sexual health.
  • Get free testing at local health centers.

“Prevention is still the best medicine.”
