About the Project

The "color calendar project" is a health awareness initiative that associates each month of the year with a specific color to highlight important campaigns and themes. The idea is to promote information, prevention, and essential care in an accessible and visually clear way.

It can be implemented in schools, companies, and other institutions to educate and promote awareness in a didactic and visual manner, engaging the public in important actions and reflections throughout the year.

The Interdisciplinary Project "Colors of Awareness" was authored by Amanda Trasmonte, Ingrid Leimig, Oscar Castro, and Luiz Cleylton.

Project Purpose

The main goal is to inform and engage the public on relevant topics, encouraging prevention, early diagnosis, and the adoption of healthy habits. Additionally, the project serves as support for educational activities and public health campaigns.

In school environments, it can be used as a pedagogical tool, allowing themes to be addressed in a playful and contextualized way. In companies, it contributes to employee well-being.

Gynecological Health

Maintaining good gynecological health is essential...

  • Schedule regular check-ups with a gynecologist.
  • Undergo routine exams, such as Pap smears.
  • Learn to perform breast self-exams.
  • Maintain a balanced diet.
  • Drink plenty of water.
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption.
  • Protect your skin from sun damage.

Mental Health

Mental health is a crucial part of overall well-being...

  • Identify signs of stress and burnout.
  • Seek professional help whenever necessary.
  • Maintain healthy social relationships.
  • Practice self-care activities.
  • Ensure adequate and quality sleep.

Physical Health

Physical health is directly linked to the proper functioning of the body...

  • Exercise regularly.
  • Maintain a balanced diet.
  • Aim for 150 minutes of physical activity per week.
  • Properly manage body weight.
  • Use sunscreen daily.
