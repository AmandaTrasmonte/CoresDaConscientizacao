Pink October - Breast Cancer Awareness Month

About

Pink October is an international awareness movement for breast cancer control, created in the early 1990s by the Susan G. Komen for the Cure Foundation.

The date, celebrated annually, aims to share information and promote awareness about the disease; provide greater access to diagnostic and treatment services; and contribute to reducing mortality.

Pink October campaigns aim to raise awareness and educate about the prevention of breast and cervical cancer, promoting early diagnosis through exams such as mammograms and Pap smears.

Actions include disseminating information, encouraging self-care, preventive exams, and alerting about risk factors.

Main Objectives

  • Awareness: Inform society about the risks of breast and cervical cancer.
  • Prevention: Encourage healthy lifestyle habits and raise awareness about risk factors.
  • Early Diagnosis: Promote preventive exams to increase chances of cure.

Common Actions

  • Lectures and events: Discussions on prevention, diagnosis, and treatment.
  • Information campaigns: Distribution of educational materials.
  • Monument lighting: Symbolic use of pink color on buildings and public spaces.
  • Health actions: Exams such as mammograms and Pap smears.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Have routine exams: Mammograms are essential for early diagnosis.
  • Adopt healthy habits: Balanced diet and regular exercise.
  • Know your body: Observe changes in your breasts and see a doctor if necessary.
