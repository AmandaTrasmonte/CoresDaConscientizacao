Blue November – Month of Awareness for Men's Health

About

The Blue November campaign is a global initiative to raise awareness among men about the importance of taking care of their health, with a special focus on the prevention and early diagnosis of prostate cancer. The movement aims to combat male prejudice against visiting doctors and performing routine exams, promoting well-being and quality of life.

Main Objectives and Focus of the Campaign

  • Main objective: Increase awareness about prostate cancer, the most common cancer among men (except non-melanoma skin cancer).
  • Early detection: Diagnosing prostate cancer at an early stage increases chances of cure to over 90%.
  • Actions: The campaign promotes preventive exams, such as digital rectal exam and blood test (PSA), while encouraging discussions about overall health, including healthy eating, exercise, mental health, and safe sex practices.
  • Combat prejudice: Aims to break barriers and the taboo many men have regarding health care.
  • Activities: Various public and private entities conduct events, lectures, and campaigns throughout November.

Risk Factors for Prostate Cancer

  • Age: Risk increases significantly after 50 years old.
  • Family history: Having a father or brother with prostate cancer considerably increases risk.
  • Race: Black men have a higher predisposition.

Exercising, eating a balanced diet, quitting smoking, practicing safe sex, caring for mental health, and performing preventive exams are essential actions to maintain men's health.
