Golden November – Month of Awareness for Childhood and Adolescent Cancer

About

Golden November is the awareness campaign for childhood and adolescent cancer, held in November. Its goal is to alert the public about the signs and symptoms of the disease, emphasize the importance of early diagnosis, and provide support to children and adolescents undergoing treatment. The golden color symbolizes the value of these children's lives.

Why the Golden Color?

  • The golden ribbon is the official symbol of the fight against childhood cancer.
  • The color represents value and preciousness — symbolizing children's lives.
  • It is also associated with light, hope, and positive energy.
  • In healthcare, it can represent “gold standard,” i.e., excellence in care and diagnosis.

Importance of Early Diagnosis

  • When diagnosed early, childhood cancer has high chances of cure.
  • Delayed diagnosis is still common due to lack of symptom awareness.
  • The campaign aims to inform families, teachers, and health professionals.

Data and Statistics

  • It is one of the leading causes of death among children and adolescents.
  • About 12,000 new cases are diagnosed per year in Brazil.
  • The most common types are leukemia, nervous system tumors, and lymphomas.

Activities during Golden November

  • Lectures and seminars for health professionals and families.
  • Educational campaigns in schools and communities.
  • Information dissemination through social media and press.
  • Charity events to support treatments and research.

How to Participate and Help

  • Share reliable information about signs and symptoms.
  • Support institutions that help children with cancer.
  • Participate in campaign events.
  • Encourage medical check-ups and regular monitoring.

November 27th is the National Day to Fight Cancer, reinforcing the message and importance of Golden November.
