Green December — Month Against Animal Abandonment and Abuse

About

Green December is an awareness campaign held throughout December to combat animal abandonment and abuse, emphasizing responsible ownership and conscious adoption. The choice of this month is due to increased abandonment during holidays and year-end festivities, promoting alerts on proper animal care.

Why Green?

  • Hope: Green represents hope for a better future for animals.
  • Awareness: Symbolizes the need for responsible ownership awareness.
  • Life: Represents respect for animal life and well-being.
  • Nature: Reflects the connection between animals and the environment.

Why December?

  • Holiday period: Many families travel and abandon animals.
  • Christmas gifts: Animals given as gifts may be abandoned later.
  • Abandonment increase: Statistics show up to 70% increase during this period.
  • Strategic awareness: Ideal time to promote conscious adoption.

Campaign Focus

Combating abandonment and abuse

  • Raise awareness about cruelty and the impacts of abandonment.
  • Warn that abandonment is a crime under Law 9.605/98.
  • Show physical and psychological consequences for animals.

Responsible ownership

  • Encourage responsible care by guardians.
  • Promote neutering to control population.
  • Highlight the importance of identification (microchip).

Reflection before adoption

  • Encourage commitment to a lifetime with the animal.
  • Warn that animals are not disposable.
  • Provide guidance on costs and responsibilities.

Reporting crimes

  • Inform how to report abuse.
  • Share reporting channels (police, environmental agencies).
  • Clarify that abandonment is a punishable crime with detention and fines.

Data on Animal Abandonment in Brazil

  • Over 30 million abandoned animals.
  • Approximately 10% of domestic animals are abandoned annually.
  • Abandonment rises up to 70% during holidays.
  • Only 1% of abandoned animals are adopted.
  • Over 170,000 animals collected in major cities annually.

How to Help and Participate

Adopt responsibly

  • Consider time, financial conditions, and space before adopting.
  • Prefer shelters and NGOs over buying animals.
  • Consider adopting adult or senior animals.

Be a responsible guardian

  • Regular veterinary care, vaccinations, and deworming.
  • Proper feeding, safe shelter, and daily attention.
  • Neutering to prevent unwanted litters.
  • Identification with microchip or tag.

Report abuse

  • File a police report.
  • Call local authorities (police) in case of immediate danger.
  • Report to environmental agencies (IBAMA) and environmental prosecutors.

Other ways to help

  • Volunteer in shelters.
  • Donate food, medicines, or supplies.
  • Share animals for adoption on social media.
  • Sponsor shelter animals.
  • Provide temporary foster homes for rescued animals.

Activities during Green December

  • Adoption fairs in parks and public spaces.
  • Educational lectures in schools and communities.
  • Free or low-cost neutering campaigns.
  • Distribution of informative materials in vet clinics and pet shops.
  • Vaccination and deworming drives.
  • Lighting public buildings in green.
  • Social media campaigns using #GreenDecember.
"Animal abandonment is a crime under Article 32 of Law 9.605/98, punishable by 3 months to 1 year imprisonment and fines. The law also criminalizes abuse, injury, or mutilation of animals."
