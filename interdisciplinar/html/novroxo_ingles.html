Purple November — Prematurity Awareness Month

What is Purple November?

Purple November is an international awareness campaign about prematurity, aimed at alerting society about babies born before 37 weeks of gestation. The main goal is to spread information about necessary care, the importance of proper medical follow-up, and the challenges faced by premature babies and their families.

Why the color purple?

  • Sensitivity: Represents the vulnerability of premature babies.
  • Individuality: Each premature baby faces unique challenges.
  • Awareness: Draws attention to prematurity.
  • Overcoming: Symbolizes the strength and resilience of the little warriors.

What is prematurity?

Prematurity is birth before the 37th week of gestation. It is divided into categories:

  • Extremely premature: Less than 28 weeks.
  • Very premature: 28 to 32 weeks.
  • Moderate to late premature: 32 to 37 weeks.

Prematurity Data and Statistics

  • Approximately 15 million babies are born prematurely worldwide each year.
  • In Brazil, there are around 300,000 premature births per year.
  • It is the leading cause of child mortality under 5 years old.
  • 1 million premature babies die annually due to complications.

Causes and Risk Factors

  • Multiple pregnancies.
  • Infections during pregnancy.
  • Diseases such as hypertension and diabetes.
  • Placental problems.
  • Maternal age very young or over 35 years.
  • History of premature birth.

Care for Premature Babies

  • NICU with respiratory support and temperature control.
  • Kangaroo method for skin-to-skin contact.
  • Breastfeeding is essential.
  • Follow-up with specialists.
  • Early stimulation.

Activities during Purple November

  • Lighting buildings and monuments in purple.
  • Lectures and informative campaigns.
  • Stories of overcoming shared on social media.
  • Fundraising events for families and research.

How to Participate and Help

  • Share accurate information about prematurity.
  • Support institutions and families.
  • Participate in campaign events.
  • Donate breast milk to milk banks.
  • Encourage pregnant women to attend prenatal care.
