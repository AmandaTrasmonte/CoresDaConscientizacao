Black November — Black Awareness

About

Black November is a month dedicated to reflecting on Black Awareness, celebrated throughout Brazil with the aim of promoting reflection on the role of black people in Brazilian society, fighting racism, and celebrating the culture, history, and contributions of the Afro-descendant population. The most important date of this month is November 20th, Black Awareness Day, which honors Zumbi dos Palmares, a symbol of black resistance against slavery.

Colors of Black November

  • Red: Symbolizes the blood shed in struggles for freedom.
  • Yellow: Represents the wealth of Afro-Brazilian culture and hope.
  • Green: Related to African roots and ancestry.

Importance of Black Awareness

  • Recognition of African contributions to the formation of Brazil.
  • Combatting structural racism.
  • Valuing Afro-Brazilian culture.
  • Promoting racial equality.

Data about the Black Population

  • 56% of the Brazilian population identifies as black or brown.
  • Black people still have less access to education and opportunities.
  • 77% of homicide victims are black.
  • The wage gap between blacks and whites is still very high.

Black November Activities

  • Lectures and debates on racial equality.
  • Cultural events of Afro music, dance, and art.
  • Historical exhibitions.
  • Educational activities in schools.
  • Marches and demonstrations for equality.

How to Participate

  • Support black entrepreneurs.
  • Consume Afro-Brazilian culture.
  • Participate in local events.
  • Fight racism daily.
