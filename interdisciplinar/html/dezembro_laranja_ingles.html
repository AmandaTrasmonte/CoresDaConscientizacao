Orange December — Skin Cancer Awareness Month

About

Orange December is the awareness month for prevention and early diagnosis of skin cancer. The campaign highlights the importance of measures such as daily use of sunscreen, protective clothing and hats, and sun exposure only at recommended times, reducing risks caused by ultraviolet rays.

Guidelines and Care

  • Use SPF 30 or higher sunscreen every day — reapply every 2 hours.
  • Avoid sun exposure between 10 a.m. and 4 p.m.
  • Wear hats, UV-protective glasses, and light clothing covering the skin.
  • Avoid artificial tanning, which increases cancer risk.
  • Monitor moles and spots — consult a dermatologist if changes occur.

Importance of the Campaign

Skin cancer is the most common type in Brazil. When detected early, non-melanoma skin cancer has over a 90% chance of cure.

The campaign aims to raise awareness about the risks of unprotected sun exposure and the importance of preventive care to maintain healthy skin.

“The color orange reminds us of the sun and warns that, despite the beauty of a tan, it can be a risk signal for developing skin cancer.”
